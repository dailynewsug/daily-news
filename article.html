Government Unveils Landmark Infrastructure Plan Worth Billions in Historic Budget Session

The sweeping development plan will see roads, bridges, hospitals and public utilities upgraded across the country over the next five years in what analysts are calling the boldest economic move in a decade.

By John Doe Daily News Correspondent
Friday 15 May 2026

Photograph: Daily News

The government has announced a sweeping infrastructure development plan that will see roads, bridges, hospitals and public utilities upgraded across the country over the next five years.

Speaking to a packed parliament, the Finance Minister outlined plans to allocate funds across five key sectors — transport, healthcare, education, energy and water.

"This is not just a budget. This is a statement of intent. We are investing in the future of every citizen, in every corner of this country." — Finance Minister

The plan has been broadly welcomed by business leaders and civil society organisations, though opposition lawmakers have raised questions about financing and oversight.
